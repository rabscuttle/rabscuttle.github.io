Summary

Cast of Characters

After running all of the models, we decided to put additional animals, or a “Cast of Characters”, through each model to see if they could correctly be identified. We defined one animal from each of the seven classes. As we reviewed the models, we began to notice the models were struggling to identify invertebrates and reptiles. More specifically, the models consistently had trouble identifying turtles. We decided to add one additional reptile, a sea turtle, to our cast of characters to see how the model would identify another turtle. The K-Nearest Neighbors, Support Vector Machine and Neural Network successfully identified the sea turtle as a reptile. While the Logistical Regression and the Random Forest identified it as a fish. See full results from our cast of characters in the first table below.

Our Best Model - Random Forest

A second table below displays training and testing scores as well as the number of animals identified incorrectly for Logistic Regression, Random Forest, K Nearest Neighbors, and Support Vector Machine. A third table displays the results for the Neural Network and Deep Neural Networks. As you can see, all of the models performed well, however, the Random Forest model resulted in the fewest numbers of animals being classified incorrectly. In addition, the training and testing scores were slightly higher than the rest of the models. Despite the fact the Random Forest failed to identify our sea turtle as a reptile (or any turtle as a reptile), we all agreed the Random Forest was our best machine learning model to categorize this data set.

Improving our Predictions

When reviewing the data set used in the project, it was clear why the models struggled most often with invertebrates, amphibians and reptiles. The data set contained only 16 amphibians, 20 invertebrates and 24 reptiles. In comparison to the 59 mammals and 40 birds. Accuracy of all of the models could be improved by adding additional reptiles, amphibians and reptiles to the dataset. And just to be sure, the additions to the data set should include a variety of turtles.
