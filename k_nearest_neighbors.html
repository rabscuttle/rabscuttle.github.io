K-Nearest Neighbor


Explanation of Model

K-Nearest Neighbors is a supervised machine learning model. It predicts the category of a data point by graphing the known data points, then determining which graphed points are closest to the unknown data point. The variable "k" determines how many neighbors the model takes into account - it then chooses the category of the majority of the neighbors to predict the category of the unknown data point.

After running a variety of options for "k", we determined that 11 appears to be the lowest value for "k" where the score is relatively stable.
